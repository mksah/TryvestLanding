How does phantom equity work?
Phantom equity is really similar to regular equity except in one critical way—it is not on the cap table. Companies that sign phantom equity contracts with their employees, contractors, or board members have no risk whatsoever of losing ownership of their company. Instead, it is an agreement that “shadows” the value of true shares of the company. That way when your Tryvestors are cashed out, they are given the fair value of those shares at the companies current valuation.

For more information, visit https://www.holloway.com/definitions/phantom-equity
Are there different types of phantom equity
Yes. There is growth phantom equity and full-value phantom equity. Full-value phantom equity is about what it sounds like. If the actual value of the shares are $5 per share, then with full-value phantom equity, the amount to be paid out would be $5.
‍
Growth phantom equity is also about what it sounds like. If the actual value of the shares are $5 per share but the phantom equity contract was signed when the shares were worth $1 per share then the value of that phantom share would be equal to $4.

Tryvest uses growth phantom equity to ensure that Tryvestors are incentivized to help the company as much as possible and still give them strong potential for upside earnings.

Why is it better than traditional equity in this system?
Investors do not like a long cap table. We have seen the effect that a non-traditional cap table can have on a potential investor—it isn’t pretty. The questions start with: is it common or preferred? How do you take votes for board decisions? Did they really provide that much value?…are all valid questions investors will ask.

The truth is, traditional equity is cheap in the short run but extraordinarily expensive in the long run and investors know that better than founders do. That’s why phantom equity is a great solution for founders to incentivize first adopters to help them spread their product and then cash them out at the next round when capital is cheaper — but not later at acquisition or IPO because then that equity is worth way more than it was when it was issued.
Is it on the cap table?
Nope! You will still retain full ownership of your company.

How much do you expect I’ll pay out?
Let’s say your company is currently valued at $10M (congrats by the way!). Then you work with Tryvest to give 1% of stockback to your customers, which results in a number of shares distributed equal to 2% of the company. Then your company starts exploding (because of Tryvest of course). If you raise at a $20M valuation then you would owe Tryvestors $400,000. Which, if you raise at a $20M valuation and sell about 15% of the company, is only 13% of the total that you raised. Pretty good deal for 250% CLV on your customers. Also, if you prefer to pay Tryvestors out 50% at a time, that can decrease the proportion of your raise given to Tryvestors. We even allow this phantom stock to be kept until an acquisition or liquidity event if you prefer to bring them on for the ride!
When do you pay out?
Whenever you want. But, you have to decide before you start so that customers know what they are getting into. We recommend cashing out at your next fundraise. Why? Because then you will have cash to pay out your Tryvestors and you will have a new valuation with which to measure growth.
